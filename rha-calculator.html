What this calculator does: Estimates how much of your employer B/C contributions will "spill" to your RHA or Active HRA due to IRS limits on the PRAP — and shows how adjusting your 401(k) contributions could reduce that spill in certain situations.

How RHA/Active HRA Spill Works

The PRAP (Pilot Retirement Account Plan) has two IRS-imposed limits that cause B/C contributions to spill out:

IRC 415(c) — Annual additions limit: Total contributions to the PRAP (B/C + profit sharing + vacation forfeiture + your 401k + post-tax) cannot exceed $72,000 per year ($80,000 if age 50+, $83,250 if age 60–63). Excess B/C contributions spill to the RHA/Active HRA.

IRC 401(a)(17) — Compensation limit: B/C contributions on pensionable income above $360,000 cannot go to the PRAP. The first $10,000 of this overage spills to the RHA/Active HRA; above $415,556, it becomes taxable PRAP Cash.

If enrolled in United's active medical plan: spill goes to your Active HRA. If not enrolled, it goes to your RHA.
